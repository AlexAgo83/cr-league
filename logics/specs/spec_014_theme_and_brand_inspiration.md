# Purpose
Define how CR League should use compact urban micro-EV as light inspiration.

The goal is a distinctive product theme, not a forced corporate skin.

# Positioning
CR League should become:

> A tactical urban micro-EV racing league where compact electric teams compete through smart preparation, risk, weather, battery management, and rivalry.

This is stronger than a generic motorsport game because it creates a clear lane:

- urban rather than grand prix super-circuit;
- electric micro-vehicle rather than F1 car;
- team strategy rather than driving skill;
- social rivalry rather than simulation realism;
- playful competition rather than corporate education.

# Inspiration Boundary
Use compact urban micro-EV as inspiration for:

- compact electric vehicle silhouette;
- urban mobility tone;
- easy-to-park / agile city movement;
- connected vehicle and software language;
- battery and maintenance vocabulary;
- modern city racing identity.

Do not assume official brand usage unless explicitly approved.

Avoid by default:

- using real partner logos;
- calling vehicles by real partner or default team names in-game;
- making the game a literal company simulator;
- overloading the product with corporate mobility messaging;
- making mechanics educational instead of fun.

Recommended approach:

- internal inspiration: compact urban micro-EV;
- player-facing universe: fictional urban racing league.

# Product Theme
The game should feel like:

- a competitive club of urban micro-EV teams;
- city circuits built around agility, weather, battery, and tight routes;
- smart fleet operators who race for glory, not traditional drivers;
- a modern arcade board game about urban racing strategy.

It should not feel like:

- Formula 1 management;
- Mario Kart slapstick;
- a corporate training tool;
- a sustainability dashboard;
- a generic car racing skin.

# Vehicle Direction
Vehicles should be fictional micro-EV racers inspired by compact urban electric cars.

Visual traits:

- small footprint;
- rounded compact body;
- friendly but competitive stance;
- readable top-down silhouette;
- strong team colors;
- subtle electric/connected details;
- no real-world licensed resemblance.

Possible internal labels:

- MicroEV;
- City Racer;
- Urban Pod;
- Compact Racer;
- Team Pod.

Player-facing naming can stay fictional and playful.

# Track Direction
Prefer urban circuits over traditional race tracks.

Examples:

- city loop;
- depot sprint;
- riverside route;
- campus circuit;
- old town technical route;
- rainy business district;
- parking structure challenge;
- dockside delivery loop.

Track traits should map naturally to the theme:

- Fast: open boulevard.
- Technical: narrow old-town streets.
- Urban: traffic furniture, tight corners, limited overtaking.
- High wear: rough pavement or stop-start route.
- Weather-sensitive: painted lanes, tram tracks, drainage issues.

# Mechanics Vocabulary
Shift some racing language toward urban electric mobility.

Prefer:

- battery;
- swap;
- eco mode;
- regen;
- grip;
- software tune;
- fleet maintenance;
- urban shortcut;
- docking zone;
- parking agility;
- telemetry;
- rain grip;
- route planning.

Use traditional racing terms only where they stay clear:

- start;
- pace;
- overtake;
- position;
- podium;
- championship.

# Card Theme Updates
Existing card mechanics can be rethemed without changing the underlying system.

Examples:

- Rain Tires -> Rain Grip.
- Mechanic Express -> Fleet Maintenance.
- Rocket Start -> Launch Boost.
- Slipstream Trap -> Urban Draft.
- Sponsor Bonus -> Fleet Sponsorship.
- Reinforced Engine -> Battery Shield or Reinforced Pack.
- Late Push -> Final Surge.
- Weather Radar -> Route Radar.

New card ideas:

- Battery Swap: cancels a late energy drop.
- Eco Mode: lower pace, higher credits.
- Urban Shortcut: bonus on technical/urban tracks, error risk.
- Software Patch: reduces one control error.
- Parking Agility: improves tight-track overtakes.
- Regen Boost: stronger late race if early pace was conservative.

Cards should still be dramatic promises, not numeric stat blocks.

# UI And Copy Implications
The UI should feel like an urban racing operations dashboard.

Useful labels:

- Next Urban GP;
- Race Briefing;
- Route Forecast;
- Team Pod;
- Fleet Status;
- Rival Target;
- Card Bay;
- Race Highlights;
- Season Loop.

Avoid too much company-like language:

- fleet efficiency;
- operational KPI;
- utilization dashboard;
- sustainability score.

Those may exist as light flavor, but the game is about competition.

# Art Direction Implications
Update art direction from "cartoon competitive retro racing" to:

> cartoon competitive urban micro-EV, lightly retro, clean, readable, modern city energy.

Visual cues:

- compact vehicles;
- urban route maps;
- painted lanes;
- charging/battery motifs;
- garage/depot atmosphere;
- bold team liveries;
- simple readable weather effects.

Avoid:

- sci-fi hover cars;
- serious F1 paddock visuals;
- corporate office aesthetic;
- over-detailed city backgrounds;
- real brand reproduction.

# First Session Implications
The first session should sell the theme fast.

Minimum first-minute signals:

- player creates an urban micro-EV team;
- vehicle preview uses compact electric silhouette;
- first circuit is clearly urban;
- briefing mentions weather/route/battery or agility;
- first card has an electric urban flavor;
- report uses rivalry and route language.

Example first GP:

- name: Riverside Loop GP;
- traits: Fast + Weather-sensitive;
- tension: "Open boulevards reward pace, but rain can turn painted lanes slippery.";
- suggested card: Rain Grip or Launch Boost.

# V1 Product Decisions
- CR League should use a fictional urban micro-EV league as its default theme.
- compact urban micro-EV is inspiration, not direct player-facing branding by default.
- Vehicle and card vocabulary should lean electric urban mobility.
- Tracks should be city routes, not classic racing circuits.
- The first vertical slice should include at least one clearly urban circuit and one compact electric vehicle visual direction.

# Non-goals
- No official partner branding without explicit decision.
- No logo usage rules in this spec.
- No legal/brand approval claims.
- No sustainability education layer in V1.
- No real-world vehicle spec simulation.
- No corporate KPI gameplay.

# Resolved Questions
- The product remains CR League for the prototype.
- Partner/internal naming stays out of player-facing copy.
- Vehicle visuals are broadly inspired by compact urban micro-EV silhouettes without reproducing a real product.
- Battery language stays theme/card vocabulary for now, not a core V1 resource.
