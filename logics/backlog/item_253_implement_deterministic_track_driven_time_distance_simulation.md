# Problem
- Chronos and classification currently come from accumulated abstract scores and replay plans, not from cars moving over a track.
- To make GP timing credible, the engine must integrate speed over circuit distance and derive finish times from crossing the race distance.

# Scope
- In:
  - Add a focused internal chrono engine module that advances each car through deterministic ticks or micro-segments over laps * trackLengthMeters.
  - Use circuit speed profiles and track zones to compute target speeds by progress, including straight/braking/corner/exit behavior and weather-adjusted grip.
  - Apply acceleration/deceleration bounds, consistency/risk variance, and deterministic seeded perturbations without non-deterministic APIs.
  - Record finish times when each car crosses total race distance and derive classification/gaps from those finish times.
  - Keep simulateRace as the public entry point and keep RaceResult compatible.
- Out:
  - Full collision physics or lane-level geometry.
  - Changing the web replay renderer.
  - Adding new runtime dependencies.

# Acceptance criteria
- AC1: Classification is derived from finish times generated by the chrono engine.
- AC2: Repeated runs with the same seed/input produce byte-identical classification, events, and replayTrace.
- AC3: All cars finish, final times are finite and monotonic by classification, and no car moves backwards.
